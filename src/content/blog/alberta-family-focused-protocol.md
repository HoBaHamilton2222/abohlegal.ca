On January 2, 2026, Alberta introduced the Family Focused Protocol (FFP), a significant change to how family law matters are handled in the Court of King's Bench. If you are considering a family law action — whether it involves divorce, parenting, child support, or spousal support — the FFP will affect how your case begins and moves through the court system.

## What Is the Family Focused Protocol?

The FFP is a set of mandatory procedures designed to reduce conflict, encourage early resolution, and ensure that the best interests of children remain central to every family law proceeding. It applies to most new family law applications filed in Alberta's Court of King's Bench.

## Key Requirements Under the FFP

**Mandatory Triage:** When a family law matter is filed, it is assessed early on by the court to determine the appropriate pathway. Cases are streamed based on complexity — some may be directed to simplified processes while others proceed on the standard litigation track.

**Family Information Session:** Parties are generally required to attend a family information session before their matter can move forward. This session provides an overview of the court process, available dispute resolution options, and resources to support families through separation.

**Early Dispute Resolution:** The FFP encourages parties to attempt dispute resolution before proceeding to a contested hearing. This may include mediation, arbitration, or a judicial dispute resolution conference. The goal is to reach agreements without the time and expense of a full trial whenever possible.

**Parenting Focus:** Where children are involved, the FFP places a strong emphasis on parenting arrangements. Parents may be required to complete a parenting course or provide a proposed parenting plan early in the proceedings.

## What This Means for You

If you are starting a family law matter in Alberta, you should expect additional steps at the outset of your case. While these steps may feel like added process, they are intended to help families reach resolution more efficiently and with less conflict.

Proper preparation is essential. Having legal counsel who understands the FFP requirements can help you meet each obligation on time and position your case for the best possible outcome.
